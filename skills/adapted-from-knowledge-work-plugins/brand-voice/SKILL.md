# Brand Voice (OpenClaw Adapted)

Define and maintain consistent brand voice across all marketing content. Document brand personality, messaging pillars, and style guidelines.

## When to Use
- Creating brand voice guidelines
- Reviewing content for brand consistency
- Adapting tone for different channels
- Documenting messaging pillars
- Enforcing style guide compliance
- Training team on brand voice

## Brand Voice Framework

### Complete Brand Voice Document

```markdown
# Brand Voice Guide: [Brand Name]

## 1. Brand Personality

**If our brand were a person:**
[Describe personality in 1-2 sentences]

Example: "A knowledgeable colleague who explains complex things simply, celebrates your wins genuinely, and never talks down to you."

---

## 2. Voice Attributes (3-5 core traits)

### [Attribute 1: e.g., Approachable]
- **We are:** friendly, clear, jargon-free, welcoming
- **We are NOT:** dumbed-down, overly casual, lacking substance
- **This sounds like:** "Here's how to get started — it takes about five minutes."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Yo! This is super easy, even a noob can do it lol."

### [Attribute 2: e.g., Authoritative]
- **We are:** confident, evidence-based, experienced
- **We are NOT:** arrogant, dismissive, inflexible
- **This sounds like:** "Based on 500+ customer deployments, we recommend..."
- **This does NOT sound like:** "Obviously everyone knows you should..."

### [Attribute 3]
[Same format]

---

## 3. Audience Profile

**Primary Audience:** [Who they are, role, expertise level]
**What they care about:** [Pain points, goals]
**How to address them:** [Tone and approach]

**Secondary Audience:** [If applicable]

---

## 4. Core Messaging Pillars

1. **[Pillar 1]:** [Key theme, why it matters to audience]
2. **[Pillar 2]:** [Key theme, why it matters to audience]
3. **[Pillar 3]:** [Key theme, why it matters to audience]

**Hierarchy:** Lead with [Pillar X], support with others

---

## 5. Tone by Channel

| Channel | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Blog | Informative, conversational | "Let's walk through how this works." |
| LinkedIn | Professional, thought-provoking | "Three things we learned from 50 campaigns." |
| Twitter/X | Punchy, direct | "Your landing page has 3 seconds. Make them count." |
| Email | Personal, helpful | "We built something we think you'll find useful." |
| Support docs | Clear, patient | "If you see this error, here's how to fix it." |

**Formatting:**

**Preferred Terms:**

|----------|----------|
```

## Voice Attribute Spectrums

Choose where your brand falls on each spectrum:

| Spectrum | One End ← → Other End |
|----------|----------------------|
| Formality | Formal, institutional ← → Casual, conversational |
| Authority | Expert, authoritative ← → Peer-level, collaborative |
| Emotion | Warm, empathetic ← → Direct, matter-of-fact |
| Complexity | Technical, precise ← → Simple, accessible |
| Energy | Bold, energetic ← → Calm, measured |
| Humor | Playful, witty ← → Serious, earnest |

---

## Tone Adaptation by Situation

| Situation | Tone Shift |
|-----------|------------|
| Product launch | Excited, confident, forward-looking |
| Incident/outage | Transparent, empathetic, accountable |
| Customer success | Celebratory, specific, crediting customer |
| Bad news | Honest, respectful, solution-oriented |
| Competitive comparison | Confident but fair, fact-based |

**Rule:** Voice attributes stay fixed, tone dials them up/down.

## Content Review Checklist

Use this to review content for brand voice compliance:

```markdown
# Brand Voice Review: [Content Title]

## Voice Attributes Check
- [ ] Matches [Attribute 1]
- [ ] Matches [Attribute 2]
- [ ] Matches [Attribute 3]
- [ ] Appropriate tone for channel/situation

## Messaging Check
- [ ] Aligns with core pillars
- [ ] Speaks to target audience
- [ ] Uses preferred terminology

## Style Guide Check
- [ ] Grammar rules followed
- [ ] Formatting consistent
- [ ] Punctuation per guidelines
- [ ] Inclusive language used

## Issues Found:
[List any violations and suggested fixes]

## Overall Assessment:
[On-brand / Needs minor revisions / Needs significant revisions]
```

## Quick Voice Templates

### Blog Post Opening (Informative Voice)
```
[Question that addresses reader pain point]

[Brief context or statistic]

[Preview of what they'll learn]

Example:
"Struggling to keep your team aligned on messaging?

When we surveyed 200 marketing teams, 73% said inconsistent brand voice 
was their biggest content challenge.

In this post, we'll walk through how to document your brand voice in a 
way your whole team can actually use."
```

### Social Post (Punchy Voice)
```
[Bold statement or statistic]

[One-sentence elaboration]

[Call to action or link]

Example:
"Your brand voice isn't what you say. It's how you say it.

We helped 50+ teams document their voice in under 2 hours.

Here's the framework: [link]"
```

### Email (Personal Voice)
```
Hi [Name],

[Personal observation or shared context]

[What you built/learned/discovered]

[How it helps them]

[Clear next step]

[Warm sign-off]
```

## Best Practices

1. **Document with examples** — Show don't just tell
2. **Define the "not"** — Prevent misinterpretation
3. **Make it scannable** — Bulleted lists, tables, clear sections
4. **Keep it accessible** — Share widely, reference often
5. **Update regularly** — Review quarterly, refine as brand evolves
6. **Train consistently** — Onboard new team members with guide
7. **Review content** — Spot-check for compliance
